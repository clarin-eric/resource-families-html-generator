Annotated tweet corpus in Arabizi, French and English Corpus

Size: 17,103 sequences

Arabizi, French, English

This corpus is aimed at studying the mechanisms studying the mechanisms of information and opinion propagation within social networks: identifying influential leaders, detecting channels for disseminating information and opinion. The purpose of the corpus constitution, completed in 2020, was to collect and annotate tweets in 3 languages (Arabizi, French and English) for 3 predefined themes (Hooliganism, Racism, Terrorism).

For the annotation, a tool running on Django has been developed in order to provide the following annotations for each tweet in a given sequence: Theme: with 5 possible annotations (Hooliganism, Racism, Terrorism, Others, Incomprehensible); Topic: the annotator can add a new topic if it does not exist in the proposed list; Opinion: 3 possible annotations (Negative, Neutral, Positive). In total, 17,103 sequences were annotated from 585,163 tweets.

The corpus is available for download from the ELRA Catalogue, although it should be remarked that the deposited data only corresponds to tweet IDS and the accompanying annotations; The original tweets can be obtained using the Twitter API.

PAISÀ Corpus of Italian Web Text

Size: 380,000 pages, 250 million words
Licence: CC BY-NC-SA 4.0

Italian

This corpus contains approximately 380,000 documents coming from about 1,000 different websites, for a total of about 250 million words. Approximately 260,000 documents are from Wikipedia, approx. 5,600 from other Wikimedia Foundation projects. About 9,300 documents come from Indymedia, and we estimate that about 65,000 documents come from blog services.

The corpus is available for download from the EURAC Research CLARIN repository.
